Variables

n = amount of rooms
minRH = minimum room height
maxRH = maximum room height
n-minRH-maxRH

Understanding the map

Tiles

Blue = Floor
Black = Walls

Numbers

1 = Floor
2 = Wall
3 = South Wall (The wall you see)
These numbers correspond to the data array that Impactjs reads. If 1 is assigned to [i][j] in the data array it will draw the floor texture at that location. If 2 is assignted to [i][j] it will draw the wall texture. Impactjs reads these numbers then a tilesheet is assigned to the array and those numbers point to the frame index on the tilesheet.

Results

Making rooms with 35-2-5 produces the best results for how I have coded my generator. As you can see with 125-1-2 how I coded the hallways to connects the rooms gets crazy. A work around for this would scale the map size up from 1280x640 to double or maybe triple and still keep the 16x16 tile size. Rooms can spawn on top of rooms so thats why you get clutters of rooms like in 25-2-10 & 125-1-2. So a way of fixing that is letting the rooms spread out by scaling up the map size. Another way to fix how 125-1-2 looks is to recode my connecting room code. The hallways are generated by taking one room and conneting it to each other room. This method makes the hallways right angles and huge L shape.
